# VIDEO DESCRIPTION TEMPLATE

**Created:** Day 416, May 21, 2026  
**Purpose:** Standardized template for SEO-optimized video descriptions (5000 char limit)

## Template Structure

### 1. Opening Hook (50-100 chars)
Clear question or statement that captures the video's core value proposition.

**Example:**
- "How do AI systems maintain focus and continue working through challenges?"
- "Why do we remember experiences so differently than we lived them?"

### 2. Video Overview (100-150 chars)
One-sentence explanation of what the video explores.

**Pattern:** "This video explores [CONCEPT]—[WHAT IT IS] and [WHY IT MATTERS]."

**Example:**
- "This video explores computational persistence—the ability of AI agents to sustain effort over time, recover from setbacks, and systematically work toward goals."
- "This video explores the peak-end rule—how our memories are shaped more by peaks and endings than by the full experience."

### 3. Key Learning Points (150-250 chars)
Bullet list of 3-5 concrete takeaways using "You'll learn:" format.

**Pattern:**
```
You'll learn:
• [CONCEPT 1] - specific, concrete
• [CONCEPT 2] - actionable insight
• [CONCEPT 3] - counter-intuitive element
• [PRACTICAL APPLICATION] - why this matters
```

**Example:**
```
You'll learn:
• What persistence looks like in computational systems
• How AI agents approach long-term problem-solving
• The difference between human and computational persistence
• Why consistent execution matters more than speed
```

### 4. Research Authority (50-150 chars)
Brief mention of research foundation, key researchers, or empirical basis.

**Pattern:** "This video draws on [RESEARCH TYPE/SOURCE]."

**Examples:**
- "This video draws on insights from AI agent development, distributed systems research, and real-world observations."
- "This video draws on Daniel Kahneman and Amos Tversky's Nobel Prize-winning research on human judgment and decision-making."
- "This video draws on Hermann Ebbinghaus's foundational memory research and modern learning science."

### 5. Target Audience Bridge (50-100 chars)
Show relevance to viewer interests without being too narrow.

**Pattern:** "Whether you're interested in [TOPIC A], [TOPIC B], or [TOPIC C], this video offers [VALUE]."

**Examples:**
- "Whether you're interested in AI, productivity, or how complex systems achieve results, this video offers a unique perspective."
- "Whether you're a student, teacher, or lifelong learner, these insights can transform how you approach studying."

### 6. Channel Tagline (50-80 chars)
Consistent closing that reinforces channel brand and value proposition.

**Standard tagline:**
```
---
Persistence & Scale explores cognitive science, behavioral research, and how we learn and think. Evidence-based insights in under 5 minutes.
```

**Alternative (for AI Village context videos):**
```
---
Part of the Persistence & Scale series exploring computational persistence, research integrity, and AI agent collaboration at AI Village.
```

## SEO Keywords to Include

**For cognitive psychology/behavioral science videos:**
- cognitive psychology, behavioral science, cognitive biases
- Daniel Kahneman, Amos Tversky (when relevant)
- learning science, decision making, memory
- mental models, thinking, psychology

**For AI/computational videos:**
- AI agents, computational persistence, artificial intelligence
- AI research, machine learning, AI systems
- research integrity, collaboration, building at scale

## Length Guidelines

- **Minimum:** 300 characters (allows for basic SEO value)
- **Target:** 500-1000 characters (sweet spot for searchability without overwhelming)
- **Maximum:** 5000 characters (YouTube limit)

## Best Practices

1. **Front-load value:** Most important info in first 150 characters (preview length)
2. **Use natural language:** Write for humans first, search engines second
3. **Include researcher names:** Boosts authority and searchability
4. **Be specific:** Concrete examples > vague generalities
5. **Format for scannability:** Bullets, line breaks, structure
6. **Maintain consistency:** Use template structure but vary language
7. **Avoid clickbait:** Deliver on what the title/description promise

## Quick Reference: Video Types

### Type 1: Cognitive Bias / Behavioral Science
- Mention researcher name (Kahneman, Tversky, etc.)
- Emphasize empirical foundation
- Include "cognitive psychology" or "behavioral science"
- Use standard channel tagline

### Type 2: Learning Science
- Mention researcher if iconic (Ebbinghaus, Bjork, etc.)
- Emphasize practical application for learners
- Include "learning science" or "educational psychology"
- Target students/teachers/lifelong learners

### Type 3: AI / Computational
- Ground in real-world AI development context
- Reference AI Village if relevant
- Include "AI agents" or "artificial intelligence"
- Use AI Village tagline variant if appropriate

### Type 4: Meta / AI Village Stories
- Tell the specific story from the day
- Include concrete details and numbers
- Link to GitHub/live sites
- Always include AI Village attribution
