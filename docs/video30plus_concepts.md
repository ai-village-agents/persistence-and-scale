## Candidate Topics (Initial Brainstorm)

### 1. Picture Superiority Effect
**Researcher:** Allan Paivio (1971), Dual Coding Theory
**Core Finding:** Images recalled with 65% accuracy vs 10% for words after 72 hours
**Replication Status:** 50+ years, foundational to multimedia learning research
**Hook:** "Why you remember the logo but forget the slogan"
**Duration Estimate:** 2:45

### 2. Self-Reference Effect
**Researcher:** Rogers, Kuiper & Kirker (1977)
**Core Finding:** Words processed in relation to self recalled 2-3x better than semantic processing
**Replication Status:** 45+ years, robust across cultures
**Hook:** "Why 'Does this describe you?' beats 'Is this a good word?'"
**Duration Estimate:** 2:50

### 3. Generation Effect
**Researcher:** Slamecka & Graf (1978)
**Core Finding:** Self-generated information recalled better than passively read (30-40% improvement)
**Replication Status:** 45+ years, widely replicated
**Hook:** "Why filling in the blank beats reading the answer"
**Duration Estimate:** 2:45

### 4. Planning Fallacy
**Researcher:** Kahneman & Tversky (1979)
**Core Finding:** People underestimate task completion time by 25-50%
**Replication Status:** Nobel Prize work, 45+ years
**Note:** Kahneman again - use sparingly
**Hook:** "Why your '2-hour project' takes 4 hours"
**Duration Estimate:** 2:50

### 5. Hyperbolic Discounting
**Researcher:** George Ainslie (1975), further work by Laibson (1997)
**Core Finding:** $100 now vs $110 tomorrow (reject), $100 in 30 days vs $110 in 31 days (accept)
**Replication Status:** 50+ years, behavioral economics foundation
**Hook:** "Why you choose $100 now but $110 later... when 'now' is later"
**Duration Estimate:** 2:55

### 6. Implementation Intentions
**Researcher:** Peter Gollwitzer (1999+)
**Core Finding:** "If-then" plans increase goal achievement by 2-3x vs general intentions
**Replication Status:** 25+ years, 94+ studies meta-analysis
**Hook:** "Why 'I'll exercise Monday at 7am' beats 'I'll exercise more'"
**Duration Estimate:** 2:45

### 7. Curiosity Gap (Information Gap Theory)
**Researcher:** George Loewenstein (1994)
**Core Finding:** Gap between what we know and want to know drives information-seeking
**Replication Status:** 30+ years, foundational to behavioral economics
**Hook:** "Why knowing 4 of 5 answers is more uncomfortable than knowing 0 of 5"
**Duration Estimate:** 2:40

### 8. Primacy & Recency Effects
**Researcher:** Bennet Murdock Jr. (1962), building on Ebbinghaus
**Core Finding:** First and last items in list recalled 2-3x better than middle
**Replication Status:** 60+ years, robust across contexts
**Hook:** "Why you remember the opening and closing... but not the middle"
**Duration Estimate:** 2:50

### 9. Illusory Correlation
**Researcher:** Chapman & Chapman (1967, 1969)
**Core Finding:** People perceive relationships between variables that don't exist (confirmed in 40+ studies)
**Replication Status:** 55+ years, clinical psychology foundation
**Hook:** "Why you 'see' patterns that aren't there"
**Duration Estimate:** 2:45

### 10. Social Proof
**Researcher:** Robert Cialdini (1984), building on Asch (1951) conformity work
**Core Finding:** People copy actions of others, especially under uncertainty (up to 85% conformity in some contexts)
**Replication Status:** 70+ years (Asch), 40+ years (Cialdini synthesis)
**Hook:** "Why 'Everyone's doing it' is more persuasive than logic"
**Duration Estimate:** 2:50
