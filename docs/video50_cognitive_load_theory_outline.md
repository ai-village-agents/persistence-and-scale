- Effect documented across mathematics, science, programming, language learning

## Core Concept
**Cognitive Load Theory:** Working memory has limited capacity (~4 items). Learning is optimized when instructional design minimizes extraneous load (irrelevant complexity), manages intrinsic load (inherent difficulty), and maximizes germane load (effort toward schema construction). Explains why worked examples beat problem-solving for beginners.

## Section 2: The Three Types of Load (0:55-1:45)
**Visual 4 (Frame 4):** Three-load framework diagram
- Title: "THREE TYPES OF COGNITIVE LOAD"
- Three stacked containers showing working memory allocation:
  1. **INTRINSIC LOAD** (white, unavoidable): "Inherent difficulty of material"
  2. **EXTRANEOUS LOAD** (gray, wasteful): "Irrelevant complexity from poor design"
  3. **GERMANE LOAD** (electric blue, productive): "Effort toward learning/schema building"
- Total height represents working memory capacity limit

**Narration:**
"Sweller identified three types of load on working memory. First, intrinsic load—the inherent difficulty of the material. Learning calculus has higher intrinsic load than learning addition. You can't eliminate this. Second, extraneous load—wasted effort from poor instructional design. Confusing layouts, irrelevant information, unclear examples. This is what you must minimize. Third, germane load—productive effort building mental schemas. This is what creates learning. Your goal: reduce extraneous, manage intrinsic, maximize germane."

**Visual 5 (Frame 5):** Worked example vs. problem-solving comparison
- Two side-by-side diagrams showing working memory allocation:
  * **Problem Solving (Beginners):**
    - Large chunk: "Searching for solution strategy" (gray, extraneous)
    - Small chunk: "Understanding concepts" (small electric blue)
    - Label: "Cognitive resources wasted on search"
  * **Worked Examples:**
    - Small chunk: "Following steps" (small gray)
    - Large chunk: "Understanding concepts" (large electric blue, germane)
    - Label: "Cognitive resources focused on learning"

**Narration:**
"Here's why worked examples win for beginners. When you solve a problem from scratch, most of your working memory is consumed searching for a solution strategy—that's extraneous load. Little capacity remains for actually understanding the concepts. But when you study a worked example, the solution is given. You're not searching—you're focusing all your capacity on understanding why each step works. That's germane load. More learning with less mental effort."

## Section 3: Real-World Applications (1:45-2:20)
**Visual 6 (Frame 6):** Practical applications across domains
- Title: "REDUCE EXTRANEOUS LOAD"
- Four examples with icons:
  1. **Teaching:** Show worked examples before practice problems
  2. **Manuals:** Integrate text with diagrams (split-attention effect)
  3. **Presentations:** Explain visuals via audio, not text (modality principle)
  4. **Software:** Minimize irrelevant UI complexity during onboarding

**Narration:**
"Cognitive Load Theory has transformed instructional design. In teaching: show worked examples before giving practice problems. In manuals: integrate text directly into diagrams—don't make readers look back and forth, that's extraneous load. In presentations: explain visuals through audio, not on-screen text—that's the modality principle, it reduces load by using separate channels. In software: during onboarding, strip away every irrelevant UI element. Anything that's not essential is extraneous load stealing working memory."

## Section 4: Practical Framework (2:20-2:40)
**Visual 7 (Frame 7):** Three-step action framework
- Title: "APPLY COGNITIVE LOAD THEORY"
- Three numbered boxes with icons:
  1. **NOTICE** when learners are overwhelmed—check for extraneous load
     Icon: Warning triangle
  2. **ASK** "What can I remove without losing meaning?"
     Icon: Scissors cutting excess
  3. **SEQUENCE** from examples to practice, simple to complex
     Icon: Stepped progression arrow

**Narration:**
"Here's your framework. First, when learners are overwhelmed, don't assume the material is too hard—check for extraneous load. Is your design adding unnecessary complexity? Second, ask what you can remove without losing meaning. Every irrelevant word, graphic, or step is stealing working memory. Third, sequence from examples to practice, and simple to complex. Don't throw beginners into problem-solving. Show them the path first."
