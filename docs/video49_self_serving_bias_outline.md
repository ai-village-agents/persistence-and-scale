## Research Authority
- Dale Miller & Michael Ross (1975): Landmark meta-analysis establishing self-serving attribution patterns
- Published in Psychological Bulletin, 2,500+ citations
- Amy Mezulis et al. (2004): Updated meta-analysis of 266 studies across 40+ years
- Effect size: d = 0.96 for success attribution, highly robust across cultures (stronger in Western cultures)
- Replicates in academic performance, sports, work outcomes, relationships, health behaviors

## Core Concept
**Self-Serving Bias:** The tendency to attribute successes to internal factors (your skill, effort, intelligence) and failures to external factors (bad luck, other people, circumstances). Protects self-esteem but distorts learning and accountability. Understanding this bias helps you extract lessons from failure and stay humble in success.

## Section 4: Practical Framework (2:10-2:40)
**Visual 8 (Frame 8):** Corrective strategy framework
- Title: "COUNTERACT SELF-SERVING BIAS"
- Three-step actionable framework:
  1. **After success: Ask "What external factors helped?"**
     - Timing, luck, support, favorable conditions
  2. **After failure: Ask "What could I control next time?"**
     - Preparation, strategy, effort, skill gaps
  3. **Track both: Keep a bias journal**
     - Record explanations, review monthly, look for patterns

**Narration:**
"Here's how to fight it. After every success, force yourself to ask: what external factors helped? Timing, luck, people who supported you, favorable conditions. After every failure, ask: what could I control differently next time? Preparation, strategy, effort, skills I need to build. And track both in a journal—write down your explanations, review them monthly, and look for the pattern. You'll catch yourself in the act."
