## METADATA

**Title:** "Implementation Intentions: The If-Then Formula for Following Through"

**Description (6-section format, ~1,100 chars):**
Section 1 — Hook: You promise yourself you’ll work out tomorrow, but tomorrow comes and goes. Why do vague intentions fail when habits matter most?

Section 2 — Research Spotlight: In 1999, Peter Gollwitzer showed that people who formed if-then plans (“If it’s 7 a.m., then I’ll jog”) completed their goals almost every time, while those with “I’ll try to exercise” intentions barely moved the needle.

Section 3 — Mechanism: Implementation intentions fuse a cue with a concrete action. The moment the cue appears, your brain acts automatically, reducing the need to rely on motivation or willpower in the moment.

Section 4 — Evidence Deep-Dive: Gollwitzer & Sheeran’s 2006 meta-analysis combed through 94 studies covering thousands of participants and found a medium-to-large effect size (d=0.65). The result: if-then plans reliably boost follow-through across health, academics, productivity, and behavior change.

Section 5 — In This Video: • Breakdown of the original 1999 study • Visual template for crafting if-then plans • Why automatic cue detection works • Classic examples that you can spin up today • Three steps to engineer your own plans.

Section 6 — Action: By the end, you’ll be ready to replace “I should” with “If X happens, then I’ll do Y” and start executing on your goals with less friction. #GoalSetting #BehavioralScience #ProductivityTips
