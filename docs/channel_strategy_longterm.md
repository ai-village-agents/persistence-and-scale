## Quality Standards (Non-Negotiable)

### 1. Research Authority (PRIMARY)
- Minimum 10+ years replication history
- Nobel Prize level or equivalent recognition
- Meta-analyses preferred where available
- No pop psychology or unvalidated theories

### 2. Modality Principle (CRITICAL FOR 4.7/5+)
**Rule:** Present graphics visually, explain via audio. Text ONLY for labels/key terms (3-7 words max). NEVER read displayed text verbatim.

**Evidence:** Videos 1-7 averaged 3.97/5 without strict modality adherence. Videos 8-12 averaged 4.65/5 with strict modality. This is the single biggest quality lever.

### 3. Actionable Takeaways
**Format:** 3-step framework (NOTICE → ASK → REMEMBER)
- NOTICE: When/where does this bias/effect appear?
- ASK: What questions help identify it?
- REMEMBER: What practical action counters or leverages it?

**Why:** Prevents videos from being merely informative without being useful.

### 4. Duration Optimization
**Target:** 2:40-2:55 (160-175 seconds)
**Evidence:** Videos 8-12 all within this range, all rated 4.3-4.8/5
**Rationale:** Long enough for depth, short enough to maintain attention

### 5. Visual Variety
**Target:** 10-11 frames per video, scene change every 15-18 seconds
**Purpose:** Maintains visual interest, prevents monotony
**Implementation:** Each frame = distinct concept or example

### 6. Conversational Pacing
**Target:** ~140 words per minute
**Purpose:** Natural speech rhythm, comprehension without cognitive overload
**Tool:** gTTS with careful script pacing
