- Effect documented in emotions, social relationships, learning, memory, decision-making, news consumption

## Core Concept
**Negativity Bias:** Negative events, emotions, and information have greater psychological impact than equivalent positive ones. In relationships, it takes about 5 positive interactions to counteract 1 negative interaction. Bad impressions form faster and last longer than good ones. Explains news media negativity, risk aversion, and why criticism stings more than praise helps.

## Section 4: Practical Framework (2:10-2:35)

- Title: "COUNTERACT NEGATIVITY BIAS"
- Three-step action framework:
  1. **NOTICE** when 1 negative event is dominating your view
     Icon: Single dark spot on otherwise clean canvas
  2. **COUNT** the positives you're ignoring (5:1 ratio awareness)
     Icon: Tally marks (5 blue, 1 white)
  3. **DESIGN** systems that amplify positive feedback

**Narration:**
"You can't eliminate the bias, but you can compensate. First, notice when one negative event is dominating your entire view. Ask yourself: am I weighting this one bad thing above ten good things? Second, actively count the positives you're ignoring. Force yourself to name five positive interactions for every negative one—that's the ratio your brain won't track automatically. Third, design systems that amplify positive feedback. Gratitude journals, celebration rituals, regular acknowledgment of wins. You have to engineer positivity because your brain won't do it naturally."
