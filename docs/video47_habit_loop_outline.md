## Core Concept
**Habit Loop:** Three-part neurological pattern that governs all habits: (1) Cue triggers the routine, (2) Routine is the behavior itself, (3) Reward reinforces the loop. Understanding this structure lets you build new habits or break old ones by manipulating specific components.

  2. **ROUTINE** (right, white box): "The behavior (physical, mental, or emotional)"
  3. **REWARD** (bottom, electric blue box): "What your brain learns to crave"

**Narration:**
"Here's the structure. First, the cue: a trigger—could be a time of day, a location, an emotion, other people, or something that just happened. Second, the routine: the actual behavior, physical or mental. Third, the reward: something your brain enjoys and starts to crave. That craving is what makes the cue trigger the routine next time."

## Section 3: How to Change Habits (1:45-2:30)
**Visual 6 (Frame 6):** Habit change strategy diagram
- Title: "CHANGE THE ROUTINE, KEEP THE CUE & REWARD"
- Visual: Two parallel loops
  * Top loop (faded): CUE → OLD ROUTINE (crossed out) → REWARD
  * Bottom loop (bright): SAME CUE → NEW ROUTINE (electric blue) → SAME REWARD
- Example labels:
  * Cue: "3 PM fatigue"
  * Old routine: "Buy coffee"
  * New routine: "10-minute walk outside"
  * Reward: "Energy + break"

**Narration:**
"Research shows the most effective strategy: keep the cue and reward, but swap the routine. If you want to break a habit, first identify what you're actually craving. Then find a new behavior that delivers the same reward. Same 3 PM fatigue, but instead of coffee, take a 10-minute walk. You still get energy and a break, but without the caffeine crash later."

**Visual 7 (Frame 7):** Building new habits framework

- Three-step visual:

**Narration:**
"To build a new habit, design an obvious cue—use an existing habit as the trigger. After I pour my morning coffee, I'll do 2 pushups. Make the routine ridiculously easy at first. And reward yourself immediately—even just checking a box gives your brain the feedback it needs to encode the loop."
