# Quality Educational Video: Research Synthesis

## Core Principles (from TechSmith + Google AI Overview)

### What Makes Quality Educational Video:
1. **Psychological engagement + clear, digestible learning objectives**
2. **Not just recording a lecture** - employ specific multimedia and psychological principles
3. **Help learners focus, retain information, and apply new skills**
4. **Respect viewers' time, use clear objectives, thoughtful presentation techniques**

### Best Practices:
- **Clear audio** - Poor sound drives viewers away faster than imperfect visuals ⚠️
- **Focused content** - Each video addresses ONE learning objective ⚠️
- **Visual variety** - Mix talking head, screen recording, animated graphics ⚠️
- **Appropriate length** - Short videos (under 12 minutes) improve retention ✓
- **Visual clarity** - Show processes step-by-step with visuals, not just describe
- **Repeatability** - Learners can pause, rewind, review

### Planning Process (Step 1):

**Define Learning Objective:**
- Center on a single, SPECIFIC outcome
- ✅ "How to log into the dashboard" (one clear skill)
- ❌ "Everything about the app" (too broad, overwhelming)
- Use action verbs and measurable outcomes
- "Explain the three steps..." not "Understand the concept..."

**Know Your Audience:**
- Tailor tone, depth, examples to learners' needs
- Consider familiarity with subject
- Consider common questions or pain points
- Match examples to everyday scenarios

### Scripting Process (Step 2):

**Keep It Simple and Focused:**
- Each script section reinforces ONE idea
- Extra details belong in separate videos
- Well-written script prevents rambling, keeps video goal-driven
- Every second should support the learning objective

**Test and Time:**
- Read script out loud - edit until conversational
- ~150 words per minute speaking pace
- 300 words = ~2 minutes
- Aim for 2-5 minutes per video segment
