## RESEARCH FOUNDATION

**Primary Source:** Zajonc, R. B. (1968). "Attitudinal effects of mere exposure." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology Monographs*, 9(2, Pt.2), 1-27.

**The Famous Study:**
- Participants viewed unfamiliar Chinese ideographs at frequencies: 0, 1, 2, 5, 10, 25 exposures
- No semantic meaning provided; focus on pure perceptual familiarity
- Participants rated favorability on a 7-point scale after exposure phase
- Result: Ratings increased monotonically with frequency; correlation ≈ .57 between exposure count and liking
- Follow-up: Similar positive shifts for pseudo-words, faces, and sounds under same exposure manipulation

**Replication:** 200+ studies across modalities (visual symbols, names, melodies, consumer goods); meta-analytic effect size d ≈ 0.48; holds across cultures and age groups; attenuated when stimuli are initially negative

**Key Insight:** Familiarity breeds liking, not contempt—mere exposure can create preference in absence of meaning or reinforcement.
